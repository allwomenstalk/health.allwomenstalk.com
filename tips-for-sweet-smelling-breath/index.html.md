# 8 Tips for Sweet Smelling Breath 247 ...

No one likes to have bad breath so we all need **tips for sweet smelling breath**. What if you have an impromptu kiss or a close conversation with a coworker in an elevator? You don't want it to smelly, do you?

One of the worst things about having bad breath is that you probably don’t even know it!

There are things you can do throughout the day, combined with regular maintenance that can get rid of your stinky breath for good. With these **tips for sweet smelling breath**, you can make sure you always smells fresh and you never have to worry about getting too close to anyone again.

## 1. Drink Lots of Water

Obviously, drinking water has lots of benefits for your body, skin, and overall wellbeing, but did you also know that it also helps with bad breath? That's right! Drinking more water is one of the best **tips for sweet smelling breath.** Try to drink at least 48 ounces of water a day and stay away from sodas, bottled iced teas and sports drinks because they are full of citric acid, which contributes to bad breath. It also wears away the enamel on your teeth. Drinking more water will keep your mouth lubricated, which keeps bacteria at a minimum, therefore cutting the stink.

## 2. Brush and Floss Every Day

Clearly, if you want to have fresh breath, you need to brush and floss regularly. For the best results, brush and floss once in the morning and once at night. Brushing helps to get the bacteria and plaque off your teeth, but flossing is absolutely necessary to get everything out that your toothbrush missed.

If you don’t floss regularly, food can end up getting stuck between your teeth which bacteria will feed on, which creates stinky breath.

## 3. Clean Your Tongue

Yes, you have to brush and floss, but don’t forget about your tongue! Brushing your tongue with your toothbrush or using a tongue scraper will remove bacteria, byproducts of bacteria, and plaque.

## 4. Use Alcohol-free Mouthwash with Zinc

A mouthwash should not contain alcohol because it dries your mouth out, leading to more bacteria and more bad breath. A good mouthwash will keep your mouth moist, which helps to wash away odor causing bacteria. Zinc also helps to kill sulphur compounds that linger in the mouth. According to Dr. Glassman, a cosmetic dentist in New York City, you can easily create your own mouthwash with a tablespoon of baking soda, one cup of warm water and a few drops of essential peppermint oil.

## 5. Take Some Probiotics

Taking probiotics can help increase the enzymes your body needs to break down foods properly and decrease the chances of food fermenting in your stomach.

## 6. Chew on Fresh Mint Leaves and Parsley

Stash some of these refreshingly scented herbs in a plastic bag for an easy on-the-go breath freshener. The fresh mint will make your breath smell good and increase your greens intake, while the parsley has chlorophyll in it, which helps fight the creation of smelly sulphur compounds.

## 7. Drink Less Coffee

Coffee has high amounts of acid, which leads to bad breath. We all know what coffee breath smells like. Avoid it by steering clear of acidic drinks to keep your body at an alkaline level instead of an acidic level.

## 8. Eat Crunchy, Healthy Foods

Eating snacks like apples, carrots, and celery will help to remove food that’s caught between your teeth and get rid of residual bacteria throughout the day. Hard, crunchy fruits and veggies act like a natural toothbrush and keep your breath smelling fresh.
