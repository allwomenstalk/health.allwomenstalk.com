# 7 Scary Women's Health Issues You Should Be Aware of ...

Women’s health issues are something that every girl should understand and take seriously. Ignoring scary problems can lead to expensive treatments and you could even lose your life. It’s important to see your doctor on a regular basis to be sure your health is in tip-top shape and that any problems are detected before it’s too late. Read through these women’s health issues so you’re aware of issues you might face.

## 1. Heart Disease

Heart disease is the top killer of women, making it one of the most important women’s health issues you should be aware of. Hormonal changes as you age are a big contributor to heart disease so your risk goes up as you near menopause. Additionally, stress plays a role, so a demanding career or family life can also be a factor. Check in with your doctor regularly to monitor your heart health and don’t be afraid to head to the emergency room if something doesn’t feel right.

## 2. Mental Health Issues

Women experience mental health problems, most notably depression and anxiety, at higher rates than men. This can be due to genetics, but hormones are also something to be aware of. If you feel down or experience high levels of anxiety, seek out a mental health professional who can help you control your condition. Depression and anxiety can lead to suicidal feelings, so it’s vital to take care of your mental health.

## 3. Sexually Transmitted Diseases

You like to think you would never choose a partner who had a shady past and might give you something. The truth is, that women are at a higher risk of complications from STDs, so your sexual health is something you should never leave up to anyone else. Practice safe sex and see your gynecologist every year to ensure that you don’t have an asymptomatic condition that could harm your fertility.

## 4. Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is probably the scariest diagnosis among women, though it isn’t the top killer of females. Keep track of your breast health yourself, but see your doctor often too. If you ever notice a lump or abnormality in your breasts, be sure to contact your physician immediately. Cancer is harder to treat if it advances, so early intervention is important.

## 5. Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is the second most common cancer among women. Yes, it’s vital to avoid cigarettes to protect your health, but even non-smokers can get the disease. If you ever feel pain, coughing or shortness of breath, talk to your doctor to rule out this scary health problem. Letting the problem go could result in the loss of part of your lungs and you may have to undergo pricey and painful medical procedures.

## 6. Alzheimer’s Disease

No, you won’t have to worry about this one until you’re older, but there are plenty of ways you can protect yourself now. Also, knowing the symptoms can aid you in recognizing the early stages. More than half the cases of Alzheimer’s affect women. Exercise and a healthy diet are great ways to keep your brain functioning properly, but stimulating your intellect by reading and doing other similar activities is also helpful.

## 7. Obesity

No girl likes talking about her weight, but it’s super important to pay attention to the number on the scale. You can look fabulous if you’re overweight, but it increases the risk of heart disease, cancer and diabetes, so it’s not a good idea to let the pounds pile on. Eat a well-balanced and healthy diet to keep your weight under control. Get plenty of exercise too.
