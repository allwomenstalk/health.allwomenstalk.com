# 10 Weird Signs of Dehydration Most People Dont Realize ...

Do you know the **signs you're dehydrated**? The most obvious sign of dehydration is of course thirst. But, it's just one of many different indicators that the body needs some water ASAP. The following are all **signs you're dehydrated** that might be a bit unexpected. Knowing what they are can help you catch dehydration sooner.

## 1. Feeling Tired

Tiredness during the day (if you slept well) is one of the classic **signs you're dehydrated**. Food isn’t the only thing you need to be ingesting to feel alert. Water is crucial for normal body function, as it makes up a good amount of your body mass. When you’re dehydrated the volume of your blood lowers, which means that the body has to work harder to pump it to the brain. When you brain isn’t being oxygenated properly, you might feel like going to sleep.

And when you take a nap you wake up even more tired. So, if you are not a regular water drinker and you find it hard to drink 7-8 glasses of water per day, you might need to find alternative ways to rehydrate your body. One way to do that, which has become very common lately, is by taking IV fluids. IV fluids are healthy liquids, containing a lot of vitamins and minerals, that give an immediate energy boost within 15-30 minutes of a procedure and hydrate your body. IV therapies are very common, by just typing [mobile IV treatment in Tulsa](https://www.hydrateyouiv.com/mobile-iv-tulsa) , or any location near you, you can find a lot of IV services.

## 2. Bad Breath

Your saliva has some natural functions, one of which is to help sweep bacteria out of your mouth. When you aren’t drinking enough water, you might experience dry mouth or lack of saliva. This allows bacteria to build up in there, and eventually, your breath will start to smell.

## 3. Cravings for Sweets

When you get dehydrated, your body might be fully aware that it needs something. It just might not know exactly how to translate what that something is. Sometimes when you get dehydrated, you actually start craving sweet stuff. This is from the failed effort of your body to break down glycogen to release it as glucose for energy. When it can’t create that energy, it assumes it needs more food, even when what it needs is actually more water.

## 4. Dry Skin

It makes sense that when your body isn’t properly hydrated your skin will begin showing signs of that. If you pinch your skin, and it’s a little more sluggish than usual as it flattens back out, this could be due to dehydration. Note this also starts to happen with age, but that’s partly because the skin doesn’t retain hydration as well.

## 5. Muscle Cramps

Muscle cramps aren’t only caused by lack of potassium, they can also occur from dehydration. Experts say that the hotter the muscles are, the more likely that they will start cramping up. Keeping them hydrated will help.

## 6. Headaches

When the body is dehydrated it can quickly lead to a headache. This might even be the cause of some migraines. Drinking water won’t always cure a headache, but it is certainly a good thing to try when one strikes.

## 7. Fevers

Severe dehydration can actually cause the body to go into a state of fever. This, in turn, will make you even more dehydrated. If you’re suddenly hit with the chills at the end of a long day in the sun, it’s worth considering when you last drank water.

## 8. A Bad Mood

Being dehydrated is enough to turn a normal day into a super irritable one. Researchers have found this mood connection to be even more significant when it comes to women. Keeping a water bottle by your side might help you down more of it if you have a hard time remembering throughout the day.

## 9. Feeling Lightheaded

If you get a lightheaded feeling when you stand up, it might be due to dehydration. When the blood volume lowers in the body, the pressure drops as well. This can have an effect on how you feel as you stand up.

## 10. Difficulty Concentrating

Super distracted? Maybe sip on some water and see if that helps. When your blood isn’t pumping through your body at full speed, the brain definitely takes a hit. This includes the focus department, as well as affecting other bodily functions.
