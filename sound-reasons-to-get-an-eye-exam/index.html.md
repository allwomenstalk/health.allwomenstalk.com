# 8 Essential Reasons You Need a Regular Eye Exam

Whether you're squinting at your phone screen or feeling like your vision is perfectly fine, the importance of regular vision health checks cannot be overstated. Reasons to get an eye exam are plentiful, yet this is an aspect of health care that is often neglected in our busy lives. If you haven't had an eye test in the last year or two, then it's time to book one now. Even if you think your eyes are doing great, regular checkups are vital for long-term wellness. Here are some important reasons to get your eyes checked today.

## 1. Driving

One of the top reasons to get an eye exam is driving. Not only is it a legal requirement to have good vision, but it's also a matter of safety for everyone on the road. It is irresponsible to put other people at risk because your eyesight isn't sufficient for driving, and it is unwise to put yourself in danger when a simple exam could solve the problem.

## 2. Computer Use

Many jobs these days require intense computer use, which can often lead to digital eye strain. It's important to take regular breaks from looking at the screen and practice good [wellness habits](https://health.allwomenstalk.com/wellness-habits-for-women/) to protect your sight. In some circumstances, employers are even liable to help you with the cost of glasses if your work involves heavy screen time. If you use a computer regularly, getting your eyes tested is a must.

Signs you might need an eye exam sooner than planned:

- Persistent headaches or eye strain
- Blurred or double vision
- Difficulty seeing at night or while driving
- Seeing flashes of light or "floaters"
- Frequent squinting to see clearly

## 3. Health Issues

Are you aware that a comprehensive eye test can detect systemic problems such as high blood pressure, diabetes, or high cholesterol? It's very likely that many people have no idea that an eye test can reveal health issues like these. This is another excellent reason to get an eye exam—the earlier these problems are detected through professional screening, the better your outcomes will be.

## 4. Diabetes

If you are living with diabetes, you should already be aware that having the condition is one of the primary reasons to get an eye exam. As mentioned, diabetes is something that can be determined or monitored during an eye test. If you have already been diagnosed, make sure that you attend [retinal screening appointments](https://www.nei.nih.gov/learn-about-eye-health/eye-conditions-and-diseases/diabetic-retinopathy) and your regular eye tests as recommended by the [American Optometric Association](https://www.aoa.org).

## 5. Unaware of Problems

Another one of the important reasons to get an eye exam is that many eye problems don't actually have obvious symptoms in their early stages, unlike problems affecting other parts of the body. Since your eyes may not let you know that there's anything wrong with them, an eye test can provide some essential clues before a condition becomes serious.

## 6. Irreplaceable

It's extraordinary how much we take our eyes for granted. But just think how important they are—without clear vision, life would be significantly more difficult. You can't get a new set of eyes, so it is crucial to look after the ones you have. Regular checkups are the best way to do that, so book that test soon!

## 7. Avoid Eye Strain

If you already wear glasses or contact lenses, you need to be sure that your prescription is up-to-date. Don't assume that it will stay the same forever. Wearing an incorrect prescription can cause significant eye strain and recurring headaches. Have an exam as often as your optician recommends to ensure you are seeing your best.

## 8. Looking after Your Body

Most people think that unless they have obvious problems with their vision, they have no reasons to get an eye exam. Look at it this way—you only have one pair of eyes, and getting a regular test will reassure you that they are working fine. And if they aren't, you will be able to take action immediately. See it as "maintenance," just like you would with a car or other [essential health routines](https://health.allwomenstalk.com/tips-for-managing-diabetes/).

| Eye Condition | What an Exam Detects | Potential Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Glaucoma | High eye pressure | Medicated drops or surgery |
| Cataracts | Clouding of the lens | Corrective surgery |
| Macular Degeneration | Retinal health issues | Vitamin therapy or injections |

There are so many important reasons to get an eye exam. It's also essential if you wear contact lenses that you are following the correct aftercare procedure and that they are not causing you any problems. Whether you wear glasses or lenses, or neither, always prioritize regular tests. When was the last time you had your eyes checked? If it has been a while, I hope this list of reasons to get an eye test has encouraged you to make an appointment soon.
