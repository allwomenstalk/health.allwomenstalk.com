# 7 Health Experts to Follow on Twitter ...

If you’re a regular tweeter, then consider following some of my favorite health experts to follow on Twitter. I was a late bloomer to the world of tweeting, yet once I started, I realized how easy and useful it was. I had just graduated from college with my nutrition degree, so I began to follow several different health experts, and I’m so glad I did. I’ve learned so much about healthy new foods and fun new exercises, found tons of great recipes, and received tips for eating on the go, eating out at restaurants, or exercising in my home. Look up some of my favorite health experts to follow on Twitter to enhance your healthy lifestyle. Professional advice is just a tweet away!

## 1. Keri Glassman, M.S., R.D

_ twitter.com_
Keri Glassman is one of my favorite nutritionists and health experts to follow on Twitter. Keri is the owner of the Nutritious Life nutrition practice in New York City, where she lives. Keri is extremely knowledgeable and smart when it comes to eating healthy. Her meals are balanced and she promotes a healthy lifestyle. She has several books out, including The Slim Calm Sexy Diet, featured by Women’s Health Magazine, and her latest book, The New, You and Improved Diet, which came out just this year. Be sure to check it out and follow Keri on Twitter for amazing recipes, health tips and nutritional advice.

## 2. The Tone It up Girls

_ twitter.com_
Karina and Katrina from Tone It Up are adored by girls and women everywhere looking to live a healthy, active and nutritious lifestyle. The Tone It Up girls are incredibly beautiful, smart, and they are also certified nutritionists and physical fitness trainers. They are not just beauty and no brains, either! Karina and Katrina are the developers of a new allergen-free vegan protein powder on the market known as Perfect Fit Protein, which is pretty amazing! They also provide readers on their blog, Tone It Up, with free exercises, recipes, workout videos, a customized nutrition plan for purchase, and more.

## 3. Dana James, MS, CNS,CDN of Food Coach NYC

![Dana James, MS, CNS,CDN of Food Coach NYC](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/health/2013/05/3_dana-james-ms-cns-cdn-of-food-coach-nyc_200x300.jpg)

[twitter.com](https://web.archive.org/web/https://twitter.com/foodcoachnyc)
Dana James rocks, ladies! Dana is a triple board certified nutritionist and founder of Food Coach NYC, a nutritional therapy practice based in Manhattan. Dana is trained in nutrition biochemistry, functional medicine and cognitive behavioral therapy. What does all that mean? Dana specializes in nutritional therapy outside of the standard medical system. She promotes natural sources of healing, and works with clients on a psychological level to understand why their health is at risk or needs attention. Her practice works with the patient to understand the root cause of the problem, not just address the symptoms. I love to follow her on Twitter since she is always touting the latest health study findings, helpful nutrition tips, recipes, and her latest blog posts, which are always inspiring. You can also find Dana’s writings on the MindBodyGreen website where she is a contributing writer.

## 4. Andrew Weil

_ twitter.com_
Perhaps one of the most popular nutritionists and doctors of all time is Andrew Weil. Weil specializes in a Mediterranean diet and practices integrative medicine, natural health and holistic healing. Weil is also a huge advocate of yoga, which I just love! Following Weil on Twitter has not only given me some great recipe ideas, nutritional facts, and yoga ideas, but has also updated me with Weil’s take on the latest medical advice, which is always interesting to hear. Weil is a pioneer in the world of health and healing, and you definitely don’t want to miss his advice.

## 5. Kimberly Snyder

_ twitter.com_
Kimberly Snyder is the New York Times bestselling author of Beauty Detox Foods & The Beauty Detox Solution. She is also a Certified Nutritionist to Hollywood's top celebrities and has been featured on Dr. Oz, The Today Show and in Vogue magazine. By following Kim on Twitter you’ll get tons of great natural detox tips, along with healthy foods to eat, Kim’s opinion on controversial health topics, sound nutrition advice and delicious recipes. Be sure to check Kim out so you can tweet your way to true beauty!

## 6. The Institute for Integrative Nutrition

_ twitter.com_If you haven’t heard of The Institute for Integrative Nutrition, they are a must-follow on Twitter. They are the largest nutrition school in the world, specializing in integrative medicine, which incorporates all aspects of a person’s life to achieve their optimal health and weight. Based in New York, the school certifies students to become Certified Health Coaches and is one of the most well-rounded, diversified programs I’ve ever studied as a nutrition student. On their Twitter page you’ll find tons of great healthy living tips, the latest diet trends and facts, tasty recipes that are great for you, and updates about the school.

## 7. Joy Bauer, M.S., R.D

![Joy Bauer, M.S., R.D](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/health/2013/05/7_joy-bauer-m-s-r-d_567x133.jpg)

[twitter.com](https://web.archive.org/web/https://twitter.com/joybauer)
Joy Bauer is a popular dietitian and the lead nutritionist on The Today Show. She is also a New York Times Bestselling author and lives in New York, where she owns her own practice. Bauer is a phenomenal health expert in my opinion. Her advice is sound and not based on trendy diet schemes. Bauer is also featured on Everyday Health’s website, and runs her own dieting program on her own website, JoyBauer.com. Through Bauer’s daily tweets you’ll get smart nutrition advice, easy recipes, nutrition and lifestyle tips, and news about all her latest books on the market.

Be sure to check out all of my top nutrition experts on Twitter so you can be your healthiest self and get free nutrition advice all while indulging in some good social media. Do you follow any nutritionists on Twitter?
