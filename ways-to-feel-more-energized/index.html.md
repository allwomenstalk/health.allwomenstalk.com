# 7 Science\-Backed Ways to Feel More Energized Every Single Day

Are you among the many women who long to know **how to feel more energized** every single day? It doesn't seem to matter how many cups of coffee you drink or how many hours of sleep you get; fatigue can feel like your worst enemy all day long. Well, you have come to the right place! In this article, you will find 7 different ways to feel more energized that actually work. I've proven it myself! Keep on reading, and before you know it, you will be feeling more refreshed and ready to take on the day.

## 1. Get Busy

The key to **feeling more energized** is to get busy and get moving! Try to fit some type of physical activity into your daily routine. Even if it's just a brisk walk, at least you will be moving. Jogging is a great way to burn calories and stay fit, but if it's not for you, consider a gym membership or trying out biking. Regular exercise is essential for long-term vitality and can significantly boost your mood.

## 2. Check Your Iron Levels

If you need to know **how to feel more energized**, start by checking your iron levels. Many times, low iron levels can cause a significant decrease in your energy. This is often linked to [iron deficiency anemia](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/iron-deficiency-anemia/symptoms-causes/syc-20355034), which is common among women. Foods that are rich in iron include fish, chicken, eggs, and beans. If you suspect you're low, talk to your doctor about an iron supplement to help restore your energy levels.

## 3. Try Aromatherapy

One of my favorite **ways to feel more energized** is to use aromatherapy! Peppermint and Jasmine essential oils are known for their invigorating and energizing properties. [Johns Hopkins Medicine](https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/wellness-and-prevention/aromatherapy-do-essential-oils-really-work) suggests that certain scents can help stimulate the senses. Try adding a few drops to your bath water, using an essential oil diffuser, or even scenting a handkerchief to take a quick whiff during a mid-afternoon slump.

## 4. Eat More Complex Carbohydrates

Complex carbohydrates provide the sustained fuel your body needs to stay energized all day long. If you want to know **how to feel more energized**, focus on incorporating complex carbs into your meals throughout the day. Options like granola bars, whole-grain bagels, whole wheat bread, and pastas are excellent choices. Your body breaks these down slowly, providing a steady stream of energy. You might also want to look into some [healthy snack ideas](https://food.allwomenstalk.com/healthy-snacks-that-will-keep-you-energized/) to keep you going between meals.

## 5. Drink Enough Water

Dehydration is one of the top causes of fatigue. If you are looking for ways to be more energized, start drinking more water! Cut back on sodas, sugary drinks, and excessive caffeine which can lead to energy crashes. While the "8 glasses a day" rule is a common baseline, staying properly hydrated is key to maintaining your over-all performance. You'll notice a huge difference in your energy levels and mental clarity when you prioritize water intake.

## 6. Avoid Excessive Sugar

If you constantly feel drained and tired, try cutting back on processed sugar. Sodas, candy, cookies, and processed snacks will do nothing to give your energy a lasting boost. Your blood sugar will spike temporarily, but it will quickly turn to insulin as your body breaks it down, leading to a crash. The end result is that you feel even more tired and sleepy than you were before! Focus on natural energy sources like fruit instead.

## 7. Figure Out the Underlying Problem

If you live a fairly healthy lifestyle and have tried everything mentioned here with no results, the problem may be something a bit more complex. Talk to your doctor to see if they can help you determine **how to feel more energized**. Persistent fatigue is often a symptom of other underlying issues, such as thyroid problems, vitamin deficiencies, or even depression. Getting a professional opinion is vital for your long-term health.

With tips like these, you will be **feeling more energized** in no time! Just remember to work on staying at a healthy weight and, of course, prioritize getting at least 7-8 hours of sleep each night. Focusing on [better sleep habits](https://lifestyle.allwomenstalk.com/tips-for-better-sleep/) is the main key to feeling more energized! Do any of our readers have some great ways to feel more energized? Please comment below and share!
