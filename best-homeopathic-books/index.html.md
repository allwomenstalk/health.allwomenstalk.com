# 7 Best Homeopathic Books ...

With homeopathic medicine and healing leaping to the forefront, a lot of people are searching for the best homeopathic books. Bearing that in mind, we've come up with a list of books written by the leaders of the homeopathic field. If you're looking for alternative medicines and natural remedies that rely on the similia similibus curentur philosophy, we've got the best homeopathic books to help you get started.

## 1. Organon of the Medical Art by Samuel Hahnemann, M.D

![Organon of the Medical Art by Samuel Hahnemann, M.D](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/health/2013/03/1_organon-of-the-medical-art-by-samuel-hahnemann-m-d_500x500.jpg)

Because Dr. Samuel Hahnemann is the father of homeopathic medicine, his tome is easily one of the best homeopathic books available. Naturally the book focuses on the treatment of the entire patient rather than individual systems. The newer versions explain everything you need to know about homeopathy in easily understood terms, with ample footnotes and glossaries to help you even further.

## 2. Health through God's Pharmacy by Maria Treban

![Health through God's Pharmacy by Maria Treban](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/health/2013/03/2_health-through-gods-pharmacy-by-maria-treban_500x500.jpg)

A lot of people who are interested in homeopathy are further interested in growing their own medicinal herbs and plants, and learning how to use them properly. Maria Treban's book offers that and more, weaving information about techniques, recipes, and advice with tales of personal experience. It's ideal for homeopathic novices, intermediaries, and experts.

## 3. The Lady's Manual of Homeopathic Treatment by Edward Harris Ruddock, M.D

![The Lady's Manual of Homeopathic Treatment by Edward Harris Ruddock, M.D](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/health/2013/03/3_the-ladys-manual-of-homeopathic-treatment-by-edward-harris-ruddock-m-d_500x500.jpg)

While some of the information in the manual might seem a little archaic or even sexist, given the fact that Dr. Ruddock published it sometime around 1900, a lot of the information is really on point and incredibly helpful. It details how to naturally deal with a variety of different problems, although I honestly haven't heard anything about green sickness since reading The Road to Wellville. All the same, there are a lot of really amazing treatments dealing with menstrual problems, pregnancy issues, headaches, and a host of other maladies.

## 4. Nature's Materia Medica by Robin Murphy

![Nature's Materia Medica by Robin Murphy](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/health/2013/03/4_natures-materia-medica-by-robin-murphy_500x500.jpg)

A new edition of this book is packed full of valuable homeopathic information, including hundreds of herbal remedies about trees, plants, and flowers, not to mention mushrooms, milks, minerals, and much more. There is information about historical uses, tales of folklore, and a host of other helpful information that will leave you intrigued and eager to learn more about homeopathic treatments.

## 5. The Biochemic Handbook by J. B. Chapman, M.D

![The Biochemic Handbook by J. B. Chapman, M.D](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/health/2013/03/5_the-biochemic-handbook-by-j-b-chapman-m-d_500x500.jpg)

If you're eager to know about what kinds of herbs, foods, and plants heal your body and treat certain diseases, this is an excellent book to get started. It revolves around cell salts, but in such a way that you'll instantly understand what Dr. Chapman is trying to explain. It's great for homeopathic professionals as well as laymen who are just getting interested in homeopathy.

## 6. Discovering Homeopathy by Dana Ullman

![Discovering Homeopathy by Dana Ullman](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/health/2013/03/6_discovering-homeopathy-by-dana-ullman_275x400.jpg)

Just as the title suggests, this is a great book for anyone who is just getting interested in homeopathic treatments. Dana Ullman strong advocates homeopathy, and you can feel that through every word. There are very detailed and descriptive explanations and discussions concerning evidence, effectiveness, and the treatment of various diseases, allergies, psychological issues, and even oral health.

## 7. Pocket Manual of Homeopathic Materia Medica & Repertory by William Boericke

![Pocket Manual of Homeopathic Materia Medica & Repertory by William Boericke](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/health/2013/03/7_pocket-manual-of-homeopathic-materia-medica-repertory-by-william-boericke_441x640.jpg)

Originally published in 1903, this repertory contains a wealth of information, much of which is still useful today. Again, because the book itself is so old, you may find some information a bit archaic, but for the most part the treatments and methods described still hold true.

If you're interested in homeopathy, you have to start somewhere, right? Any one of these books will do the trick, but I recommend buying two or three of the ones that seem the most appealing to you, and make sure you choose at least one earlier tome. How do you feel about homeopathic methods and medicine?
