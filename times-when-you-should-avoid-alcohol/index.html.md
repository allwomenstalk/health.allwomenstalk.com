# 7 Times When You Should Avoid Alcohol

There are a number of times when you should avoid alcohol. While many of us enjoy an occasional drink, it is not always safe or advisable. In these situations, you should opt for a non-alcoholic alternative. Here are seven times when it is best to skip the alcohol.

## 1. Medication

One of the primary times to avoid alcohol is while taking medication. Some drugs, including certain antidepressants, can intensify the effects of alcohol or lead to dangerous side effects. Always check the patient information leaflet that comes with your prescription and speak with your doctor or pharmacist if you are unsure whether it is safe to drink while on your medication. For more information on managing your health, visit the [NHS website](https://www.nhs.uk/).

## 2. Driving

It is never worth the risk to drink and drive, even if you feel fine. Your judgment and reaction times can be impaired after even a small amount of alcohol. Beyond the legal consequences of losing your license, the most important reason to abstain is the safety of yourself and others on the road.

## 3. Flying

While some travelers have a pre-flight drink to settle their nerves, it is often a counterproductive choice. The effects of alcohol are frequently intensified at high altitudes due to lower oxygen levels and the dehydrating environment of an airplane cabin. It is better to pass on the alcohol both before and during your flight to ensure you arrive feeling your best.

## 4. Pregnancy

Guidelines regarding alcohol consumption during pregnancy are clear: there is no known safe level of alcohol to consume while pregnant. Opting for non-alcoholic beverages is the safest choice for both you and your baby. For guidance on a healthy pregnancy, check the latest recommendations from the [American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists](https://www.acog.org/).

## 5. Going Home Alone

If you are heading out for the night, always prioritize your safety, especially if you plan to travel home alone. Maintaining your awareness and judgment is essential when navigating your way back. If you are interested in more safety tips, check out our guide on [how to stay safe on a night out](https://health.allwomenstalk.com/how-to-stay-safe-on-a-night-out/).

## 6. Preparing for a Big Event

If you have an important meeting, exam, or event the following day, alcohol can negatively impact your sleep quality and leave you feeling sluggish. Skipping the wine or cocktail tonight will ensure you are rested, focused, and ready to perform your best tomorrow.

## 7. You Simply Do Not Feel Like It

It is perfectly okay to skip a drink just because you do not want one. You do not need a specific reason to abstain, and you should never feel pressured to drink in social situations. Prioritize how you want to feel and stick to your own boundaries.
