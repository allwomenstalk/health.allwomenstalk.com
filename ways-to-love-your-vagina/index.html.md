## 4. Special "K"

The other "K". I'm talking Kegels. Developed by Dr. Arnold Kegel in 1948, Kegel exercises strengthen and tighten the pelvic muscles which support the uterus, bladder, small intestine and rectum. Pregnancy, childbirth, being overweight, abdominal surgery and aging, cause the pelvic muscles to weaken creating health risks and problems such as urinary incontinence, uterine prolapse and dysfunction of the Pubococcygeus ("PC" ie: pelvic floor muscle). The cool thing is, unlike other kinds of exercise, you can do Kegels discreetly almost anywhere! You can't do pull-ups on the subway, (okay, you CAN but don't!) but you can do Kegels! So there goes that, "I don't have time" excuse. Identify your PC muscles - the muscles you use to hold in pee - then with an empty bladder, tighten them and hold the contraction for 5 seconds, then release for 5 seconds. Try doing 10 reps, 3 times a day. You can even work up to contracting for 10 seconds, then releasing for 10 seconds. And trust me, just like any other exercise, it is so worth it!
