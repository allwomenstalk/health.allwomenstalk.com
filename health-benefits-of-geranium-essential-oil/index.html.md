# 7 Health Benefits of Geranium Essential Oil ...

Although geranium essential oil is not widely used, there are still many benefits of geranium essential oil. Geranium oil has a pleasant floral scent that is slightly lemony. While this oil does smell good, it also has many health benefits that come from chemical compounds that are found in it, such as citronellol, geraniol, and linalool. Because of these and other compounds, there are quite a few benefits of geranium essential oil.

## 1. Antimicrobial

Geranium essential oil has the ability to kill bacteria and fight fungus. In fact, it has been used by Native Americans for hundreds of years as a natural way to treat dysentery and heal cuts. While it has been used by Native Americans as an antimicrobial agent for many years, researchers have recently taken an interest in geranium oil and found that it is effective against at least 25 strains of bacteria and can even fight yeast infections. Clearly, its antimicrobial activity is one of the many benefits of geranium essential oil.

## 2. Heal Scars

If you have a scar that you would like to disappear, you should try using geranium essential oil. Geranium oil has the ability to heal scars and make them fade because it increases blood circulation and promotes even distribution of skin pigments. To fade a scar with geranium essential oil, you need to mix it with a little coconut oil and massage the oil into the scar. Don’t expect results overnight, but it should fade over time.

## 3. Stops Bleeding

One of the amazing attributes of geranium essential is that it has the ability to help wounds clot by increasing your blood's ability to coagulate. Not only does it help blood clot, geranium oil also causes blood vessels to contract, which will make the bleeding stop faster. Because if its ability to stop bleeding, geranium essential oil is a great oil to keep in a first-aid kit.

## 4. Diuretic

As a natural diuretic, geranium oil helps the body eliminate unwanted toxins by increasing urination. In addition to helping get rid of unwanted toxins, the diuretic qualities of geranium oil will also help eliminate water retention. Since many women struggle with water retention during their monthly cycle, geranium oil can be very beneficial oil for women.

## 5. Deodorant

While you can purchase deodorant at the drugstore, not everyone likes the potentially harmful ingredients that are found in some deodorants. Yet, finding an alternative can be tricky. Geranium essential oil offers an alternative to traditional deodorants. Its light floral scent actually gets stronger when you perspire, and the antibacterial properties can help keep odor causing bacteria at bay.

## 6. Promotes Healthy Skin

Geranium essential oil is a great oil for skin because it can be used for many different skin complaints. It helps treat acne by fighting the bacteria that cause acne. It also decreases wrinkles by causing skin to tighten. Geranium oil can even be helpful in treating skin infections because it kills both bacteria and fungus.

## 7. Decrease Risk of Neural Degeneration

Neural degenerative diseases like dementia and Alzheimer’s can be very difficult to cope with, and researchers are constantly looking for ways to decrease the risk of these diseases. One method of decreasing the risk of these diseases is to use geranium oil, which activates cells that fight the inflammation in neural pathways that can cause neural degenerative diseases.

While it may not be one of the most common essential oils that people use, geranium oil certainly has many health benefits. It can help everything from skin quality to wound healing, which is quite amazing and may have you considering using geranium essential oil. Have you ever used geranium essential oil?
