# 7 Warning Signs of Breast Cancer to Be Aware of ...

Breast cancer. It’s something that women try to avoid thinking about as much as possible, but it’s also something that we all need to be fully aware of if we are going to catch potential **warning signs of breast cancer** early for the best possible outcome.

As part of Breast Cancer Awareness Month, thousands of sites across the web are doing their bit by giving helpful tips and pieces of advice to all women, both young and old, in the hope that if even one person notices a problem, then a huge difference will have been made. Here are some potential **warning signs of breast cancer** that you should be looking out for regularly, not just because it’s Breast Cancer Awareness Month.

## 1. Warmth or Swelling

Does one of your breasts feel slightly warmer than usual without there being a specific cause or reason? Have you noticed a slight or significant swelling in your breast area anywhere from the armpit to the nipple? These are things that should really be checked out by a doctor and are one of the most common potential **warning signs of breast cancer.**

## 2. New Lumps

Any kind of new lump, hardening, or thickening in the armpit or breast area is cause for concern. Most of the time, these are just natural changes that will come and go in your body, but as soon as you notice a lump of any shape or size, you should make an appointment with your doctor to have it checked out.

## 3. Redness or Flaky Skin

Have you noticed that your breast skin is redder or flakier than normal? You might think that it simply a reaction to the new bra your bought or the swimsuit that you have been wearing, but it could be something more than that.

## 4. Puckering or Dimpling

If you have noticed any physical changes such as puckering or dimpling on the surface of your breasts, then this can be a tell tale sign of something being wrong.

## 5. Nipple Pain

Don’t mistake that nipple pain that you have been experiencing as a result of your daily jog or of a badly fitting bra. If it hurts all the time and even more so when slightly pulled, it really should be checked out.

## 6. Nipple Discharge

Following on from nipple pain, if there is nipple discharge, you need to see a doctor. Any grown woman knows that nipple discharge if you are not nursing a child is not normal, so don’t hide away. Be brave and get seen by a doctor as soon as possible.

## 7. General Breast Pain

A large percentage of women experience some degree of hormonal breast pain as a part of their normal menstrual cycle, but if you have been feeling more pain than usual and at a time when your menstrual cycle might not be the reason, then it is highly recommended that you seek the advice of a medical professional.

The thing to remember with all of these signs is that the sooner you see a doctor, the sooner you can be diagnosed and the sooner any treatment that is needed can take place. Don’t be afraid or embarrassed to visit the doctor. They have seen it all before and want to help you live a long, healthy life.
