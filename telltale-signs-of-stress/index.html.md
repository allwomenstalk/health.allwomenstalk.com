# 9 Telltale Signs Your Body Is Stressed

If you suspect you're dealing with stress, it's time to check in with yourself. We live in a fast-paced world, and while some stress is a normal part of life, there comes a time when we need to pause and reevaluate. We aren’t always aware when we’re pushing ourselves too hard. In fact, many symptoms of chronic stress don’t match the classic signs we’ve all heard about. Take a look at these telltale indicators so you can prioritize your [mental well-being](https://health.allwomenstalk.com/how-to-manage-stress-daily/) and seek support if needed.

## 1. Hair Loss

One of the most noticeable physical signs of stress is hair loss or premature graying. While thinning hair can have many causes, stress is a major trigger. The good news, [according to the American Osteopathic Association](https://aoa.org/), is that hair loss caused by stress is often temporary and tends to resolve once the underlying stressor is addressed.

## 2. A Weakened Immune System

Constantly catching colds? A weakened immune system is a common consequence of chronic stress. While your body has built-in mechanisms to regulate immunity, the prolonged release of stress hormones can actually hinder your body's ability to fight off infections. You can help support your immune function by prioritizing consistent sleep, balanced nutrition, and regular movement.

## 3. Chronic Anxiety

Occasional worry is a normal reaction to deadlines or busy schedules, but frequent, overwhelming anxiety may be a signal that your stress levels are too high. If you find yourself in a cycle of worry that persists even when your workload lightens, consider talking to a healthcare professional about generalized anxiety disorder. Support is available, and you don’t have to manage it alone—[the Anxiety & Depression Association of America](https://www.adaa.org/) offers great resources to get started.

## 4. Headaches

Headaches are incredibly common, but if you're experiencing tension headaches more frequently, it may be your body telling you it’s time to slow down.
