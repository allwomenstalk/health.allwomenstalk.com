# Why You Actually Need Probiotics: 7 Science\-Backed Reasons for Gut Health

We’ve all heard the word "probiotics" buzzing around the wellness world, but do you actually know what they do for your body? While the concept has been around for decades, modern science is finally catching up to just how essential these tiny powerhouses are for our overall health. From boosting your immunity to clearing up your skin, the benefits of [gut health](https://health.allwomenstalk.com/gut-health-tips/) go far beyond just "good digestion."

Probiotics are live [microorganisms](https://www.nih.gov/) that are considered "good" bacteria because they help maintain the health and integrity of your digestive system. Most people have a negative view of microorganisms and bacteria, thinking they only cause disease. In reality, they are a vital component of fighting and preventing illness by keeping your internal ecosystem in balance.

Here are 7 reasons why you need to integrate probiotics into your daily [wellness routine](https://health.allwomenstalk.com/daily-wellness-habits/).

## 1. Your Immune System Needs and Craves Them

Nearly 80% of your immune system resides in your digestive system, so it is essential to supplement properly with probiotics in order to maintain proper health and function. This is one of the most important reasons why you need probiotics for long-term vitality.

## 2. Probiotics Improve Digestive Function Considerably

When there is an imbalance of good and bad bacteria in the gut, you can become susceptible to various health problems. Keeping things balanced ensures your body can absorb nutrients efficiently and keep your energy levels steady.

## 3. Probiotics Have the Ability to Reverse and Prevent Certain Diseases

The research is promising: probiotics can help manage or prevent conditions like ulcers, IBS, Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, and even psoriasis. By supporting your gut, you're giving your body a better chance at resilience.

| Digestion | Aids in nutrient absorption | Less bloating |
| Mental Clarity | The gut-brain connection | Improved mood |

## 4. Probiotics Prevent Negative Side Effects of Antibiotics

Antibiotics are life-saving, but they often destroy the microflora community in the digestive system. This disruption can lead to multiple issues, such as ulcers or secondary infections. Probiotics help repopulate the "good guys" after a round of medication.

## 5. Probiotics Protect the Digestive System from Harmful Pathogens and Toxins

They act as a shield, counteracting the negative reactions that toxins cause in the body. By strengthening the gut barrier, they prevent harmful substances from entering your bloodstream.

## 6. Probiotics Prevent the Overgrowth of Candida in the Digestive Tract

While candida is naturally present in small amounts, it can cause significant health problems when it grows in excess. Probiotics keep this yeast in check, maintaining a healthy microbial balance.

## 7. Probiotics Can Improve the Quantity and Quality of Your Sleep

Better sleep is something we could all use. The [gut-brain axis](https://www.sleepfoundation.org/physical-health/gut-health-and-sleep) plays a major role in regulating our sleep cycles, and a healthy gut often leads to a more restful night.

So why are probiotics such a critical component of being healthy? As I mentioned before, 80% of our immune system is located in our digestive tract. That is a massive portion of our health dictated by our gut! Do you find that you get sick frequently, don't sleep well, have chronic stomach problems, or always feel tired? Many people who suffer from chronic illness and maladies such as chronic fatigue, psoriasis, thyroid problems, and various other conditions don't realize that their problems are originating in their digestive systems.

Studies from the [NIDDK](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/) have shown that between 60 and 70 million Americans are directly affected by digestive diseases. To put that into perspective, nearly a quarter of a million people in the U.S. die every year from [digestive related diseases](https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/fastats/digestive-diseases.htm). That’s 250,000 every year—a number we can help lower with better gut care.

**Fun Fact:** There are ten times as many microbes in your body as human cells! Over time, scientists have connected over forty diseases to bacterial imbalances in the digestive system. Connections have been made between these imbalances and conditions like IBS, depression, anxiety, arthritis, thyroid problems, and mood disorders. There are even definitive links between cancer and bacterial health in the gut. It has been proven that taking probiotics and properly caring for the body can decrease the risk of developing certain cancers.

Research has proven that even the slightest imbalance between good and disease-causing bacteria in the digestive system can lead to detrimental chemical imbalances in the brain, which can cause mood disorders. In general, people who regularly take probiotics are less anxious, less depressed, sleep better, have more energy, and are generally healthier than those who don't. Every physiological system in our body relies on the health of your digestive system.

Quick Tips for Better Gut Health:

- Incorporate fermented foods like yogurt, kefir, or kimchi into your meals.

- Stay hydrated to help your digestive system process nutrients.

- Prioritize fiber-rich vegetables to feed your good bacteria.

- Manage stress, as it directly impacts your gut flora.

Whether you realize it or not, you ingest toxins on a daily basis. While it's not intentional, it's virtually impossible to avoid in our modern environment. Many of these toxins harm our immune and digestive systems, prohibit them from functioning optimally, and can cause inflammation throughout the body that can lead to all kinds of chronic illnesses and conditions. Our digestive system is essentially our "second brain," and it's absolutely crucial for us to nurture and take care of it to the best of our abilities.

So where do you go from here? What do you need to do in order to ensure that your body is functioning the way that it’s intended to? Finding a high-quality probiotic and taking it every day is one of the simplest and most effective changes you can make. It's as simple as that!
