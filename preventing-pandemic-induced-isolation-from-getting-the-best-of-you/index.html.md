# Preventing Pandemic\-Induced Isolation from Getting the Best of You ...

As you well know, the ongoing coronavirus pandemic has resulted in the implementation of social distancing policies in many communities across the globe. This change has led many to experience feelings of depression and loneliness. Some people even find themselves increasingly vulnerable to emotional distress and addictive behaviors.

With this in mind, it’s important to take steps to prevent the pandemic-induced isolation from getting the best of you. Consider using the following five tips to help yourself cheer up and resist engaging in destructive behaviors during isolation:

## 1. Stay in Touch Using Technology

Even when you cannot enjoy close physical contact with other people, you still have a great opportunity to converse with them online or over the telephone. Make a point of communicating with family members, neighbors, and friends this way. You'll help dispel a sense of loneliness and isolation by speaking frequently with other people. Maintaining your social networks remotely also helps keep the current temporary disruption in perspective. You may need to practice social distancing, but you don't need to cut yourself off from other people!

## 2. Address Any Addictive Behaviors Responsibly

Have you noticed an urge to engage in addictive behavior recently? Some people respond to stress by overeating, consuming too much alcohol, or engaging in gambling or substance abuse. Addiction [treatment centers in Orange County](https://www.northboundtreatment.com/locations/california/orange-county/) and other areas under lockdown have made adjustments in order to continue helping people during the pandemic. Don’t wait to seek well-qualified professional services if you find yourself in need of treatment for addiction.

## 3. Make Sure You Obtain a Good Night's Sleep

Sometimes people discover they either sleep too much or they [lose sleep](https://health.allwomenstalk.com/things-to-help-insomniacs-sleep-at-night/) when isolation occurs. Discuss either of these problems with a qualified physician. By ensuring you rest soundly every night, you'll help your body cope more effectively with the stress of social distancing. Obtaining adequate rest assists people in maintaining better health during periods of disruption.

## 4. Eat Well Balanced, Nutritional Meals

By paying close attention to nutrition, you'll assist your body in coping with the epidemic more effectively. It may make sense to ask your family doctor to recommend a quality vitamin supplement, for example. Additionally, focus on preparing [well-balanced meals](https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/nutritionsource/2020/03/25/food-safety-nutrition-and-wellness-during-covid-19/). Not eating (or eating too much) can indicate stress during this disruptive period. Try and maintain balance in your life by ensuring you eat nutritious meals at this time.

## 5. Enjoy Physical Activity

Even if you can't attend fun social events during periods of mandatory physical distancing, you still have a great opportunity to remain active and busy. Pay attention to the mundane pursuits that offer some healthy exercise and help you stay focused, such as gardening, house cleaning, or training a pet. Remaining active on a daily basis helps you alleviate tension. It also lets you keep in better shape during periods of compulsory stay at home orders!

## 6. Enduring a Challenging Period

Coping with the stress of social distancing might appear personally challenging right now. Yet it proves important to remember this health crisis won't last indefinitely. At some point, social gatherings will resume around the world.

By taking steps to prevent isolation from getting the best of you during this unusual historic event, you'll gain satisfaction knowing you've done everything possible to help the community. People who practice social distancing right now protect the health of the most vulnerable people in society. They deserve everyone's thanks!
