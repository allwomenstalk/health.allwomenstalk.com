# 7 Amazing Health Benefits of Quercetin You Should Know

The flavonoid quercetin is one of many flavonoids that offer numerous health benefits. Flavonoids are natural plant pigments acting as antioxidants, which neutralize free radicals that can cause damage to your cells. This particular compound has been widely studied and proven to be especially beneficial for overall wellness. While you can purchase supplements to reap the benefits of this healthy flavonoid, it is abundantly found in many fruits and vegetables. This provides all the more reason to [embrace a plant-based diet](/ways-to-transition-to-a-plant-based-diet/).

Here is a quick overview of why quercetin is so important:

- Acts as a powerful antioxidant

- Helps fight inflammation

- Supports immune system function

## 1 Many Plants Have Quercetin

I am sure you have heard the phrase, "an apple a day keeps the doctor away." One of the reasons apples are so healthy is because they contain the flavonoid quercetin. However, they are not the only source. Blueberries, sweet potatoes, kale, onions, and cranberries all contain quercetin, making them just a few of the many excellent plant sources available. You can even get a healthy dose by incorporating buckwheat into your meals. If you eat a lot of fruits and vegetables, you will naturally consume a good amount of quercetin, which, as we will explore, carries some incredible health benefits.

## 2 Natural Antihistamine

Consuming foods rich in quercetin is a highly effective way to keep seasonal allergies at bay. When you experience an allergic reaction, your body releases histamines. This response triggers a runny nose, itchy eyes, hives, and other unpleasant symptoms. Generally, people turn to over-the-counter medications to relieve these issues. However, as noted in [point 1](#1), eating foods packed with this nutrient serves as a natural alternative. It actively prevents your immune cells from releasing histamine in the first place, offering relief without the drowsy side effects. For more details on histamine response, you can check resources from the [American Academy of Allergy, Asthma & Immunology](https://www.aaaai.org/).

## 3 Keeps Your Heart Healthy

Quercetin and other essential flavonoids help maintain your heart health by decreasing the risk of arterial plaque buildup. It can also significantly lower LDL (bad) cholesterol and help manage blood pressure levels. By keeping plaque buildup, cholesterol, and blood pressure in check, quercetin actively works to prevent serious conditions like heart attacks and strokes. Supporting your cardiovascular system is always a smart choice for long-term health.

## 4 Decreases the Risk of Cancer

Researchers have discovered that flavonoids can effectively hinder the growth of cancer cells. Quercetin, in particular, has been shown to slow or stop the growth of cancer cells in the ovaries, breasts, colon, and prostate. It is considered especially helpful for decreasing the risk of lung cancer. One notable study demonstrated that it even reduced the risk of lung cancer in individuals who smoked more than 20 cigarettes a day. While it is never a substitute for medical treatment, its protective properties are well-documented by institutions like the [National Cancer Institute](https://www.cancer.gov/).

## 5 Helps Keep Bones Strong

You can help keep your bones strong by relying on quercetin to stimulate bone growth and decrease the breakdown of bone mass. It effectively increases osteocalcin, a critical hormone needed for proper bone formation. It also decreases the activity of osteoclasts, which are the cells responsible for resorbing bone tissue. Clearly, this is an essential flavonoid for maintaining healthy, dense bones as you age.

## 6 Beneficial for Autoimmune Diseases

Autoimmune diseases like rheumatoid arthritis and lupus occur when the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own healthy cells. For example, the immune system targets the joints in people with rheumatoid arthritis, causing significant joint damage and pain. Quercetin can be incredibly beneficial for managing autoimmune diseases because it has a natural modulating effect on the immune system. This allows it to decrease the aggressive activity of an overactive immune response and provide some much-needed relief. Taking care of your immune health is crucial, much like [recognizing the signs of a weak immune system](/signs-you-have-a-weak-immune-system/).

## 7 You Can Have Too Much

As wonderful as this compound is, it is definitely possible to have too much of a good thing. This typically happens when people rely heavily on concentrated supplements rather than whole foods. Taking high doses, usually more than one gram per day, can potentially cause kidney damage and lead to a loss of proper protein function. Therefore, it is always much better and safer to get your daily intake by eating a wide, colorful variety of fruits and vegetables.

Quercetin remains one of many flavonoids that can dramatically benefit your health. Not only does it decrease frustrating allergies, but it also supports your bones, protects your heart, and can even help manage complex autoimmune diseases. To ensure you are getting plenty of this nutrient to enjoy these benefits, try simply eating an apple a day. Did you know just how powerful and healthy quercetin really is?
