# 7 Raw Juices to Try This Fall to Reboot Your System and Help You Feel Brand New ...

Juicing has been a popular trend for years on end now, with tons of raw juices on the market and raw juice recipes to make at home. Though juice fasting is an effective diet method, I don’t recommend it if you suffer from hypoglycemia, or if you’re looking for long term weight loss. Instead, I think juicing should be added into a normal healthy eating plan, instead of supplemented for meals. That being said, I do highly believe in the benefits of juicing, and have experienced them firsthand myself. Fall and spring are the two best times to reboot the body with freshly made raw vegetable juices. Why so? We tend to eat heavier in the summer through cookouts, ice cream outings, and in the winter we tend to eat heavier comfort foods when we’ve been indoors most of the year. This makes spring and fall the best times to implement juice as a natural energizing and cleansing enhancement to a healthy eating and exercise plan. In Ayurvedic medicine, it is also said spring and summer are the body’s rejuvenation seasons and juicing is a great way to enhance this natural process. Try these fabulous raw juices from the website Reboot With Joe, which is a website dedicated to juicing by the famous Joe Cross, author of the book “Fat, Sick & Nearly Dead.” His story is remarkable since he cured himself from being overweight and ill, all through juicing. You can read more about him in the sources section of this article, but for now, pull out your juicers or blenders and make these raw juices!

## 1. Twist of Lime with Fennel Juice

![Twist of Lime with Fennel Juice](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/08/29081631_613_550x672.jpg)

This is one of the best raw juices of all because it is so different and effective than most recipes out there. This fabulous zesty juice is rich in potassium, digestive friendly nutrients, and enzymes that aid removal of waste and help eliminate toxins from the body, along with helping your body absorb nutrients better from other foods. Try this refreshing mix of unique ingredients this fall when your system needs a nice recharge!

**Ingredients:**
2 fennel bulbs
6-8 kale leaves (Tuscan cabbage)
2 limes

**Directions:**
Wash all produce.

Peel the lime (optional).

Chop the ingredients to fit through the juicer.

Juice and enjoy! Pour over ice if desired.

**Substitutions:**
Fennel – celeriac root, celery, kohlrabi, jicama, daikon, apple

Kale – cucumber, celery, kale, zucchini, watercress, chard (silverbeet), spinach

Lime – lemon, grapefruit

## 2. Mint to Be Green

![Mint to Be Green](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/08/29081631_447_534x800.jpg)

Oh, I just adore this juice! Mint is one of my favorite ingredients to juice with. It is relaxing, aids digestion, promotes a sense of clarity and well being, and it freshens your breath as a bonus! When paired with these other cooling, cleansing ingredients, it makes a fabulous juice to try in the morning or afternoon!

**Ingredients:**
1/4 honeydew melon
2 celery stalks
1/2 cucumber
1/2 lime
1 handful mint

**Directions:**
Wash produce well.

Cut honeydew flesh from rind.

Cut cucumber and lime in half.

Juice all ingredients except lime. I squeezed the lime juice into the juice but you can cut peel off of the lime and run it through the juicer as another option.

Serve over ice as desired and enjoy!

**Substitutions:**
Honeydew – cantaloupe, other melons

Celery – jicama

Cucumber – zucchini

Lime – lemon, ginger

Mint – basil

## 3. Rockin Melon Roll

![Rockin Melon Roll](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/08/29081631_801_553x833.jpg)

Melons are great to cleanse the system and a great source of low sugar fruits if you’re sensitive to [fruits high in sugar](https://food.allwomenstalk.com/best-fruits-to-eat-for-a-low-fructose-diet/). Since melons are mostly water and potassium, they are fabulous for preventing dehydration. Try this delicious melon blend, which is rich in gut-boosting benefits as well as relieving exercise-induced muscle pain and fatigue.

**Ingredients:**
1/2 cantaloupe (rockmelon)
4 – 6 leaves rainbow chard (silverbeet)
1 apple
1 handful of fresh mint leaves

**Directions:**
Remove rind from cantaloupe.

Wash all ingredients well.

Add produce through the juicer, pour over ice and enjoy!

**Substitutions:**
Melon – any type of melon

Rainbow chard – kale or spinach

Apple – pear

Mint – basil

## 4. Blood Orange Juice

![Blood Orange Juice](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/08/29081631_299_549x756.jpg)

One of my favorite types of juice to make is juice with beets and blood oranges. These rich, red colored oranges are a great source of Vitamin C and beets are rich in antioxidants that aid in oxygen production and flow to the brain and muscles. Beets also help to detox your liver and aid in natural cleansing. Oh, and who can forget carrots? They are naturally sweet to aid in sugar cravings, while still providing tons of magnesium, Vitamin A for clear skin and Vitamin C as well. Drink this juice anytime you need an energy boost or need to get in a little extra immunity power!

**Ingredients:**
4 blood oranges
1 beet (beetroot)
2 carrots

**Directions:**
Peel blood oranges and beets.

Rinse the carrot and beets.

Add all produce through juicer and enjoy!

**Substitutions:**
Blood orange – orange, mandarin, ruby grapefruit

Beet – red cabbage, golden beets

Carrot – sweet potato, winter squash, pumpkin

## 5. Bye Bye Blues Juice

![Bye Bye Blues Juice](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/08/29081632_253_497x750.jpg)

Oh, don’t we all just love blueberries, or is that just me? Blueberries are rich in antioxidants that are great for your entire body. They are also full of Vitamin C and properties that may aid in weight loss. Along with being great for your heart, blueberries also aid in reducing stomach inflammation that can occur from physical or emotional stress, which makes this juice so beneficial. Jicama is a veggie that is wonderfully low on the glycemic index, and rich in Vitamin C. Cucumbers add a nice cleansing source of potassium, while lime adds a zesty touch to the sweetness of the fiber-rich pears in this juice.

**Ingredients:**
1/2 cup/600 g blueberries
1/2 jicama
1/2 – 1 cucumber
1 lime
1 pears

**Directions:**
Peel the lime.

Cut all items small enough to fit in juicer chute.

Juice everything and enjoy!

**Substitutions:**
Blueberries – any type of berry

Jicama – beetroot or ginger

Cucumber – parsley or zucchini

Lime – lemon or orange

Pear – apple

## 6. Super Green Detox Juice

![Super Green Detox Juice](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/08/29081631_994_427x640.jpg)

If you ate too many things from your neighborhood barbecue, or one too many ice cream cones this summer, then you may need a serious green dose of detox juice. This recipe is no joke, girls! It offers a delicious taste, despite the rich green color that might turn you off at first glance. I promise after you savor this elixir and the way it makes you feel, you’ll change your mind about the vibrant green hue if you're hesitant at first. This juice will eliminate all sources of acid, toxins and harmful irritants from the digestive tract while providing the body with an array of beneficial nutrients. So remember, the greener, the better!

**Ingredients:**
6 kale leaves (Tuscan cabbage)
2 large handfuls of parsley
1 large cucumber
2 celery sticks (plus leaves)
1 zucchini
1 lime

**Directions:**
Peel lime for a less bitter flavour.

Wash all vegetables.

Add produce through your juicer and enjoy!

**Substitutions:**
Kale – broccoli, zucchini, lettuce, spinach, chard (silverbeet)

Parsley – arugula (rocket), coriander

Cucumber – Celery, zucchini, jicama

Celery – Cucumber, zucchini, jicama

Zucchini – Green beans, chard(silverbeet), green pepper (capsicum)

Lime – yellow grapefruit, lemon

## 7. Splash of Sun

![Splash of Sun](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/2013/08/29081631_687_484x640.jpg)

Just because summer is over doesn’t mean you can’t keep a little sunshine juice in your life! This juice is perfect for fall because it reminds me of all the wonderful fall colors that come alive at the peak of the season. It also brings your body to new life with digestive enhancing ginger, nutrient rich carrots, and Vitamin C rich oranges, which are all available at their peak in the fall season. This juice is great for reducing inflammation, giving you a boost of energy and giving your skin a dose of sunshine as well. Plus, just look at that gorgeous color! You’re sure to feel prettier just drinking it!

**Ingredients:**
3 large carrots
2 oranges
1 large slice ginger root

**Directions:**
Wash carrots and peel oranges.

Slice ginger.

Add all ingredients through the juicer.

Pour over ice (optional)

**Substitutions:** none

If you don’t have a juicer, feel free to use your blender, though the effect won’t be as great as if you use a juicer. You’ll need to add about ½-1 cup water with the ingredients into your blender, blend on high, and strain through a sieve or nut milk bag to release the juice.
